Interesting Results

Unsurprisingly, it was a "black box" regression method, the Random Forest method, which performed best. Minimizing mean-squared error the most among the different methodologies.

However, not all is lost. Our linear regression, with its many different features, arrived surprisingly close to the mean-squared error. Even better, such results performed significantly better than other linear regressions that used far more features. This is keeping in mind that the phenomena we were attempting to predict was a non-linear phenomena (electricity usage).

The lesson to take away here is that, with enough crafty transformations, we can linearize phenomena that is usually non-linear. While it might be better at regression than methods such as random forests, this allows us to have some more oversight over the regression formula than we would with a machine-learning method such as random forest.
